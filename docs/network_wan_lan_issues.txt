Title: WAN/LAN Connectivity Troubleshooting (MPLS, Leased Lines, PRI)

Issue: Branch office or head office reports slow or dropped connectivity to central systems.

Step 1: Identify Scope
- Single user: likely a local LAN cable, switch port, or NIC issue.
- Entire branch office: likely a WAN link issue (MPLS/Leased Line down or degraded).
- Voice/calling issues specifically: likely a PRI line issue, separate from data WAN.

Step 2: Local LAN Checks
1. Confirm the affected PC's network cable and switch port with a known-working cable/port swap test.
2. Check switch port status lights - amber/blinking often indicates a duplex mismatch or partial link issue.
3. Run `ping <default gateway>` from the affected machine to confirm local LAN connectivity before assuming a WAN problem.

Step 3: WAN Link Checks (MPLS/Leased Line)
1. Check the WAN router's link status LED and interface status (via router console or management interface).
2. Ping the WAN provider's near-end and far-end IP addresses (provided by the ISP) to isolate whether the issue is on the customer side or provider side.
3. Check for recent configuration changes on the router (QoS policies, routing table changes) that may have caused the outage.
4. If the link is confirmed down on the provider side, log a ticket with the ISP/MPLS provider and reference the circuit ID.

Step 4: PRI (Voice) Specific Checks
1. Check the PRI card/interface status on the EPBX system.
2. Confirm channel status - if all channels show busy/unavailable but call volume is normal, this may indicate a signaling issue with the provider rather than a hardware fault.
3. Test with a manual outbound call to confirm whether the issue is inbound-only, outbound-only, or both.

Escalation:
For confirmed provider-side WAN or PRI issues, log a ticket with the vendor immediately and keep the circuit ID and issue timestamp documented for SLA tracking purposes.
